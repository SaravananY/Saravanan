Hello!

I am a Ph.D. student at King Abdullah University of Science and Technology (KAUST), working in the areas of partial differential equations, mean-field games (MFG), and market microstructure. I expect to receive a Ph.D. from KAUST in December 2022, under the direction of Professor Khaled N. Salama. This website is currently under construction, so dont take any information from here as serious.
My research interests are in optimal control theory, nonlinear partial differential equations, MFG, and market microstructure. MFG is a framework to study interactions of a large number of indistinguishable players that play differential games. MFG has become an active research area with applications in financial and industrial engineerings, economics, crowd dynamics, and more. Moreover, market microstructure is an area of finance concerned on how trading mechanisms affect the price formation process. My interests range from active subjects of applied mathematics, such as MFG, to empirics of high-frequency finance. I aim at developing novel applications of optimal control and MFG theory to market microstructure, aligned with numerical, empirical, and practical aspects of financial markets.
